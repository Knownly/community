title: Making a NowNowNow page author: Dwight Gunning date: 25-01-2016 headline: Share the things that you're focused on right now img_src: guides/nownownow/img/nownownow.jpg img_attr: nownownow.com {% extends "guide.html" %} {% block css_head %} {{ block.super }} {% endblock %} {% block body %} {% filter markdown:"fenced-code-blocks" %} _Now pages_ are a neat initiative started by [Greg Albritton](http://gregalbritton.com/now) and [Derek Sivers](https://sivers.org/now). They are a simple variation on the ubiquitous 'about' and 'contact' pages you see all over the web. The point of the 'now' page is to share what you're focussed on right now, at this stage of your life. For more of back-story and aims of the project, feel free to jump over to [nownownow.com](http://www.nownownow.com). This guide is written for folks who've never created a webpage from scratch but are keen to do so. It's just six simple steps that will take roughly 15 minutes. _If you're already up and running with Knownly, feel free to skip to [Step 4](#now-page-template) and grab our 'now' page template._ ## 1. Sign up and install Dropbox First up you'll need to sign up for a Dropbox account and install the app. This will allow you to work on your website on your laptop and the Dropbox app will take care of sending your files to Dropbox in the cloud. With your website files in Dropbox, Knownly can make them accessible as a website. Just head over to [Dropbox](https://www.dropbox.com) to register. After you have your Dropbox account, you'll want to install the Dropbox app on your laptop or PC. Instructions should show up immediately after you register, but in case they don't, here's a [direct link](https://www.dropbox.com/install). ## 2. Register with Knownly Knownly registration is simple: All you'll be asked to do is to grant the Knownly service permission to setup a new area within your Dropbox. This is where you can create a website folder that will hold your _now_ page. Once you've authorized Dropbox, the final step is to select your Knownly plan. Now you're up and running! Go do it. ## 3. Create a Knownly Website After you've got your Knownly account, you'll be shown the Knownly Console where you can manage your websites. It's time to create your website. That's just a matter of deciding what domain you'd like to use, and the submitting it via the form in the Knownly console: ![Create website form]({% static '/guides/nownownow/img/create_knownly_website.png' %} "Knownly Console") Shortly after receiving confirmation in the Knownly console that your website has been created, a new folder for your website will be created within your Dropbox folder on your machine. Inside you'll see a placeholder file called "index.html". ![Dropbox Recent Changes shows 'index.html']({% static '/guides/nownownow/img/dropbox_recent_changes.png' %} "Dropbox Recent Changes") You can type your chosen website domain into your web browser and this HTML page will be displayed. Congratulations on getting the first page of your website online! ## 4. Add the 'now' page template As you'll expect, we need to do some extra work to create a new 'now' page. To follow the 'now' page convention, we need to creat a page that will be accessible from a url that looks like: _http://mysite.knownly.net/now/_ Knownly sites, like many websites, treat URLs that end in a '/' as a shorthand for the 'index.html' in the corresponding directory. This is often simply referred to as the "index" file for a website or website sub-directory. Whenever visitors use a URL that ends in '/', Knownly will look for and serve the file named 'index.html' in that directory. !['now' sub-directory and index.html ]({% static '/guides/nownownow/img/now_page_folder_structure.png' %} "Website structure") Now on to creating your now page: 1. Create a sub-folder named 'now' within your Knownly website folder. It should sit along side the placeholder 'index.html' that was automatically generated for the website. 2. Inside the 'now' folder, create a new file called index.html 3. Add the HTML template (shown just below) so you can quickly test out your 'now' page 4.
